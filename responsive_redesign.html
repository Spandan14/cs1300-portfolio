[0] what's wrong?

Providence Noodle Bar is a relatively new restaurant in downtown Providence. I personally frequent the establishment, and my friends and I have always left super pleased!

One of the key features of their menu is the setup of pairings, which are the set of side dishes and toppings that come with every plate to enhance the dish.

But this is one of the key things that the online menu misses out on! The menu is just a simple list of dishes, and the wall of text below every image bores the reader, misleading them into thinking that this is just another restaurant.


It doesn't end there - here's a more comprehensive analysis:

Criteria Strengths Weaknesses
Efficiency clear categorization no search or filtering, inconsistent formatting
Learnability straightforward vertical layout unclear dietary labels, add-on pricing obscured
Memorability consistent branding vanilla and standard visuals, no distinctive elements
Conceptual Model logical grouping of sides, entrees, etc. obscured menu structure (pairings), limited interactivity, long portions of text
Accessibility missing alt text, low contrast, distracting background

The lack of accessibility is particularly concerning - it might look okay on an initial WAVE scan, but multiple images have no alternative text, just an empty string. Additionally, the dietary restrictions note has very low contrast, and the background image's colors are very close to the solid white backgrounds of the cards.

[1] implementation hour

I started by creating a Figma file with the original menu, and then I created a new page for my redesign. I used the original menu as a reference, but I also took inspiration from other menus that I liked, particularly that of Mokban, which is another restaurant in the area.

This table will help summarize the main the edits I made.

Old New Why?
static layout, not perfectly responsive enhanced 2-column/1-column responsive layout a more mobile-friendly layout just helps customers browse the menu more easily! who's bringing their laptop with them?
cards with backgrounds and shadows, and a background image plain white background with a grid layout the cards get in the way of responsiveness and don't add any info
walls of text describing menu items, small dietary restriction note at the end per-dish breakdown of pairings, dietary restrictions, add-ons, etc. no user wants to read a wall of text to figure out what they're getting. the newer, more playful layout also draws customers into understanding the menu
Montserrat as the font of choice, everywhere a balanced usage of serif and sans serif fonts not only does it add more flair, it hits home the cozy vibe that PVD noodle bar is going for at their venue, which was previously missing in the website
no image interactions image overlays with magnified view onclick users are able to see the image more clearly, see what they're ordering, and this also makes the menu more accessible to the visually impaired

[2] the final product

The redesign is a lot nicer visually, and is much more accessible than the previous menu. Nevertheless, there's a lot that can still be added to this. For example, I omit the lunch menu in this design, as it wouldn't be too much different from the main menu, and PVD might want this to be a separate page anyway. You can check it out here!
